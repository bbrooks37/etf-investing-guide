Investment Glossary

Here are some common terms you'll encounter in the world of ETFs and investing:

ETF (Exchange Traded Fund)

A type of investment fund that trades on stock exchanges, much like a stock. It typically holds a diversified portfolio of assets such as stocks, bonds, or commodities.

Diversification

The strategy of spreading your investments across various assets to reduce risk. ETFs inherently offer diversification.

Expense Ratio

The annual fee charged by an ETF or mutual fund, expressed as a percentage of your investment. Lower expense ratios are generally better.

NAV (Net Asset Value)

The per-share value of an ETF's underlying assets. ETFs trade throughout the day, and their market price can sometimes deviate slightly from their NAV.

Liquidity

Refers to how easily an asset can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price. Highly liquid ETFs can be traded quickly.

Volatility

A measure of how much an investment's price fluctuates. Higher volatility means greater price swings.

Bull Market

A period in which stock prices are generally rising and investor confidence is high.

Bear Market

A period in which stock prices are generally falling and investor confidence is low.

Index Fund

A type of mutual fund or ETF that tracks a specific market index, such as the S&P 500. They aim to replicate the performance of the index.

Dividend

A payment made by a company to its shareholders, usually out of its earnings. Many ETFs that hold dividend-paying stocks will pass these dividends on to their investors.
